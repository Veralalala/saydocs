# Risk disclosure

This section explains where loss can still happen, even when insurance features exist.

Use it before you assume a policy will pay, a refund will be immediate, or market stress will not affect settlement timing.

***

### What this section covers

#### Scenarios outside coverage

Explains the common situations where insurance does not pay, or where the policy ends through cancellation or refund instead:

* [**List of scenarios not covered by insurance**](list-of-scenarios-not-covered-by-insurance.md)

#### Extreme-market funding pressure

Explains how the platform can handle payout queues, underwriting pauses, and temporary capacity limits during extreme volatility:

* [**Instructions for handling insufficient funds in extreme market conditions**](instructions-for-handling-insufficient-funds-in-extreme-market-conditions.md)

***

### What to keep in mind

* Insurance coverage has trigger rules and exclusion rules.
* A policy status alone does not always prove that funds already arrived.
* Market stress can change timing, but not the formal rule hierarchy.

***

### Read this section if you want to know

* when a policy does **not** pay
* when a payout or refund can be delayed
* what evidence to rely on during settlement
