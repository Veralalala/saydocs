# Instructions for handling insufficient funds in extreme market conditions

### Purpose

Extreme volatility can create temporary pressure on payout or refund capacity. This page explains the general handling principles when that happens. The formal agreement and accepted policy terms always control the final outcome.

***

### General principles

* The platform can pause new underwriting, queue payouts, or apply other risk controls during abnormal market stress.
* Existing policy records, trigger records, refund records, and account ledger entries remain the basis for later settlement.
* A policy marked `triggered` or `refunding` does **not** always mean funds have already arrived.

***

### What can happen during stress events

#### Temporary payout delay

Payouts can remain pending until the payout switch is reopened or funding capacity is restored. Front-end status alone is not final proof of receipt.

#### Temporary underwriting restrictions

The platform can disable new purchases, renewals, or market-level coverage for some products or symbols.

#### Processing states before final transfer

A policy can move through states such as `triggered`, `refunding`, or `cancelled` before the final ledger or on-chain movement completes.

***

### What users should rely on

* Check your policy status, refund events, payout records, and account ledger entries.
* Follow in-app notices, official documentation, and public announcements.
* Do not rely on screenshots, chat messages, or third-party statements as proof of settlement.

***

### What does not change

* The formal agreement still governs the scope of obligations and the handling order.
* Governing law and dispute rules still follow the agreement you accepted.
* Wallet security remains your responsibility. Never share your private key or mnemonic.
